Fast Facts and Stats

More children grow up to become bilingual or multilingual than monolingual in the world.4
According to the US Census Bureau’s 2008-2012 American Community Survey1:

  1. Over 300 languages are spoken in the United States
  2. In the US, 20.9% of the population speaks a language other than English
  3. In California, 43.8%
  4. In Los Angeles County, 56.8%
  5. In the City of Los Angeles, 60.1%
  6. In Orange County, 45.6%
According to a 2015 US Census Bureau survey2:
  1. With a population of 291,484,482 people over the age of 5:
  2. 60,361,574 people speak a language other than English at home
  3. Of this group, only 41.71% are classified as “Speak[ing] English less than ‘Very Well’”
  4. 37,458,624 people speak Spanish
  5. 10,737,607 people speak Indo-European languages (including French, Italian, Portuguese, German, Yiddish, Greek, Russian, Persian, etc.)
  6. 9,539,099 people speak Asian and Pacific Island languages (including Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Vietnamese, Tagalog, etc.)
